Adjustable Gastric Banding

Adjustable gastric banding is a less invasive procedure compared to other bariatric surgeries. It involves placing a silicone band around the upper part of the stomach. This procedure reduces stomach size and limits the amount of food that can be consumed. The band is adjustable without the need for additional surgery, making it a suitable option for those seeking a less invasive solution.

Adjustable Gastric Banding Procedure

The surgery is performed laparoscopically, where the surgeon makes several small incisions in the abdomen and places an adjustable silicone band around the upper part of the stomach, creating a small pouch.

The band can be adjusted by injecting or withdrawing fluid through a small port placed under the skin. The procedure typically takes between one to two hours and is performed under general anesthesia. After the surgery, the incisions are closed, and the patient begins recovery under close medical supervision.

Benefits of Adjustable Gastric Banding

  • Adjustability without additional surgery.
  • Less complex compared to other surgeries.
  • Reversibility of the procedure.

Medical Instructions for Adjustable Gastric Banding

  • Follow a liquid diet after the surgery.
  • Avoid solid foods until the band is adjusted.
