Laparoscopic Hernia Repair

Laparoscopic hernia repair is a surgical procedure used to strengthen the abdominal wall and repair hernias without the need for large incisions. This procedure is less painful and allows for faster recovery compared to traditional open surgery. It is an ideal option for individuals suffering from inguinal or umbilical hernias, offering effective results with a reduced risk of hernia recurrence.

Laparoscopic Hernia Repair Procedure

The surgery is performed under general anesthesia, where the surgeon makes small incisions in the abdomen to insert a small laparoscope and a tiny camera.

The hernia site is precisely identified, and the protruding tissue is returned to its natural position inside the abdomen. Then, a surgical mesh is placed to strengthen the abdominal wall and prevent future hernias.

The mesh is secured using staples or small sutures, and the small incisions are closed using sutures or surgical glue.

The procedure typically takes between 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the size and complexity of the hernia. After completion, the patient is transferred to the recovery room for monitoring before being discharged from the hospital.

Benefits of Laparoscopic Hernia Repair

  • Small incisions with minimal scarring.
  • Faster recovery compared to open surgery.
  • Reduced risk of infection.
  • Less post-operative pain.
  • Effective in preventing hernia recurrence.

Medical Instructions for Laparoscopic Hernia Repair

  • Avoid lifting heavy weights for 4-6 weeks after the surgery.
  • Follow a light diet in the first few days to avoid constipation.
  • Light movement after the surgery to promote blood circulation.
  • Follow-up with the doctor after two weeks to ensure proper recovery.
  • Report any severe pain or abnormal swelling in the surgical area.
